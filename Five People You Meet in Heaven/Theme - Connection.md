# Theme - Connection

> *"There are no random acts. That we are all connected. That you can no more separate one life from another than you can separate a breeze from the wind."*

## Definition

Every life is intertwined with others in ways we cannot see. Actions that seem isolated create ripples that affect strangers, sometimes across decades. What appears random is part of a larger pattern.

## Evidence from the Text

### The Blue Man's Death
Young Eddie chases a baseball into the street. Joseph Corvelzchik swerves to miss him, has a heart attack, and dies. Eddie lives. They are strangers, yet one's survival requires the other's death.

### Nicky's Key
A teenager loses a car key at an amusement park. Months later, an 83-year-old man dies because of it. They never met.

### Ruby Pier's Creation
Emile builds a pier to make his wife happy. Decades later, Eddie works there, meets Marguerite, and dies there. Emile and Ruby never knew Eddie.

### The Chain of Accidents
The Brighton gondola accident, the Wonderland Park tragedy — Eddie knows these stories and lives in fear of them. His vigilance prevents similar disasters at Ruby Pier.

## Why It Matters

The theme challenges the modern sense of isolation. It suggests:
- You matter more than you know.
- Your smallest actions have consequences.
- Strangers are not truly strangers — they are part of your story.
