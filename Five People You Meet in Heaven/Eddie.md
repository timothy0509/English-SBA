## Biography

### Childhood
Eddie grew up at [[Ruby Pier]] in the 1920s–1930s. His father worked maintenance at the park, and Eddie spent his youth running along the boardwalk, playing baseball, and dreaming of bigger things. His father was neglectful and violent, leaving emotional scars that would last a lifetime.

### Youth & Love
At 17, Eddie met [[Marguerite]] at the Stardust Band Shell. They fell in love dancing to "You Made Me Love You." Eddie dreamed of studying engineering and building things, but his plans were interrupted by World War II.

### The War
Eddie enlisted and fought in the [[War in the Philippines|Philippines]]. He was captured along with [[The Captain]], Smitty, Morton, and Rabozzo. They endured months in a POW camp, forced to mine coal. Eddie used his juggling skills to distract the guards and help them escape. During the escape, Eddie tried to run into a burning hut to save what he thought was a child, but [[The Captain]] shot him in the leg to stop him — saving his life but crippling him forever. The Captain died moments later on a land mine.

### Return & Marriage
Eddie returned home with a permanently damaged leg. He married [[Marguerite]], but their plans to adopt a child were ruined after Marguerite was severely injured in a car accident caused by teenagers dropping a bottle from an overpass. Eddie blamed himself and fell into a darkness that never fully lifted.

### Working Life
After his father died, Eddie took his maintenance job at Ruby Pier to support his mother. He stayed there for decades — fixing rides, checking bolts, listening for trouble in the machinery. To the outside world, it seemed like a nothing life. But Eddie's vigilance prevented countless accidents.

### Death
On his 83rd birthday, a cart on Freddy's Free Fall malfunctioned (caused by [[Nicky]]'s lost car key). After the passengers were evacuated, the cart dropped. Eddie saw a little girl ([[Amy or Annie]]) in its path. He lunged, pushed her to safety, and was killed by the impact. His final words: **"Get back!"**

## Eddie in Heaven

Eddie's afterlife journey is the spine of the novel. He meets five people across five distinct "heavens":

1. **[[The Blue Man]]** at a 1920s [[Ruby Pier]] — learns about [[Lesson 1 - Connection|Connection]]
2. **[[The Captain]]** in the Philippine hills — learns about [[Lesson 2 - Sacrifice|Sacrifice]]
3. **[[Ruby]]** in a snowy mountain diner — learns about [[Lesson 3 - Forgiveness|Forgiveness]]
4. **[[Marguerite]]** at endless wedding receptions — learns about [[Lesson 4 - Love|Love]]
5. **[[Tala]]** at a river of children — learns about [[Lesson 5 - Purpose|Purpose]]

Each meeting peels back a layer of regret and reveals the hidden meaning of his life.

## Key Relationships

- **[[Marguerite]]** — The love of his life. He never stopped loving her, even after her death.
- **[[Eddie's Father]]** — Complicated. Eddie hated him, yet still craved his approval. He learns the truth about his father's final night in heaven.
- **[[Eddie's Mother]]** — Source of warmth and tenderness in his childhood.
- **[[Joe]]** — His brother. Successful salesman. Their relationship was competitive but not hostile.
- **[[Dominguez]]** — Young coworker who admired Eddie. He inherits Eddie's position.
- **[[Mickey Shea]]** — Family friend whose actions indirectly caused Eddie's father's death.
