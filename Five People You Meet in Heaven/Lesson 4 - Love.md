# Lesson 4: Love

> [!note] The Lesson
> *"Lost love is still love, Eddie. It takes a different form, that's all. You can't see their smile or bring them food or tousle their hair or move them around a dance floor. But when those senses weaken, another heightens. Memory. Memory becomes your partner. You nurture it. You hold it. You dance with it. Life has to end. Love doesn't."*

## Teacher

**[[Marguerite]]** — Eddie's beloved wife

## Context

Eddie's fourth heaven is a series of wedding receptions. He walks through doors into different cultures, languages, and traditions — all celebrating marriage. In an Italian village, a bridesmaid offers him candy-covered almonds:

> *"Per l'amore e il dolce? For the bitter and the sweet?"*

It is Marguerite.

## The Lesson Explained

### 1. Love Survives Death
Eddie believed that when Marguerite died, his love became pointless. She teaches him the opposite: love changes form, but never ends.

### 2. Memory Is Love
When you can no longer touch, see, or hear the person, memory becomes the vessel of love. It is not a replacement — it is love itself, transformed.

### 3. Eddie's Love Was Felt
Marguerite tells Eddie she felt his love even after death, even in heaven. "That's how strong lost love can be."

### 4. The Bitter and the Sweet
Marriage — and love itself — contains both. Marguerite's basket of almonds represents this duality. Eddie must accept both to fully love.

### 5. The Final Dance
Marguerite saves a private reception. Eddie asks her to appear not as the young bride, but as she was at the end — 47, with lines around her eyes and thinner hair. They dance to "You Made Me Love You." He tells her he wants to stay. She vanishes, leaving him with the memory.

## Real-World Application

- Grief is not a sign that love is over — it is proof that love was real.
- Honoring memory is an active, ongoing form of love.
- Love does not require physical presence to be powerful.

## Key Quote

> *"You had to live without love for many years, didn't you?"*

> *"You felt that it was snatched away, that I left you too soon."*

> *"No, you didn't. I was right here. And you loved me anyway."*
