## Role in the Story

Mickey Shea is a complex figure — simultaneously a fun-loving family friend and the catalyst for tragedy.

### The Fun Uncle Figure
At Eddie's 5th birthday, Mickey flipped Eddie upside down for "birthday bumps," a traditional Irish custom. Everyone laughed. He was part of the pier's social fabric.

### The Dark Side
Mickey was an alcoholic. He slept through shifts. He was fired for drunkenness. In despair, he went to Eddie's mother for help — but his loneliness turned into assault. Eddie's father chased him, intending to kill him.

### The Tragedy
Despite Mickey's betrayal, Eddie's father saved him from drowning in the ocean. The hours in the freezing rain gave Eddie's father pneumonia, which killed him. Mickey never forgave himself.

## Significance

Mickey embodies **the destructive power of addiction** and **the complexity of loyalty**. Eddie's father hated Mickey in that moment, yet couldn't let him die — because years earlier, Mickey had vouched for him at the pier and lent money when Eddie was born.
