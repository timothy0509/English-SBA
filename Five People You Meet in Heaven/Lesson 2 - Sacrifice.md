# Lesson 2: Sacrifice

> [!note] The Lesson
> *"Sacrifice is a part of life. It's supposed to be. It's not something to regret. It's something to aspire to... Sometimes when you sacrifice something precious, you're not really losing it. You're just passing it on to someone else."*

## Teacher

**[[The Captain]]** — Eddie's commanding officer in World War II

## Context

Eddie's second heaven is the Philippine battlefield. He meets his old Captain, who reveals two devastating truths:
1. The Captain was the one who shot Eddie in the leg.
2. The Captain died moments later on a land mine.

Eddie attacks the Captain in fury. The Captain lets him — then explains.

## The Lesson Explained

### 1. Sacrifice Is Universal
Every person sacrifices. A mother works so her son can go to school. A daughter moves home to care for her sick father. A man goes to war.

### 2. Eddie's Sacrifice
Eddie lost his leg, his dreams of engineering, his mobility. But he gained his life. The Captain sacrificed Eddie's leg to give him the rest of his years.

### 3. The Captain's Sacrifice
The Captain died clearing a path for his men. He might have lived if he hadn't run ahead. But "no one gets left behind."

### 4. Passing It On
> *"Rabozzo didn't die for nothing, you know. He sacrificed for his country, and his family knew it, and his kid brother went on to be a good soldier and a great man because he was inspired by it."*

Sacrifice creates a chain of inspiration and survival.

### 5. Don't Focus on the Loss
Eddie's bitterness came from thinking about what he lost. The Captain teaches him to think about what was given.

## Real-World Application

- Parents sacrificing for children are not "losing" their lives — they are passing them forward.
- Caregivers, soldiers, teachers, and maintenance workers all make daily sacrifices.
- The value is not in the size of the sacrifice, but in the love behind it.

## Key Quote

> *"I took your leg to save your life."*

> *"I got to keep my promise. I didn't leave you behind."*
