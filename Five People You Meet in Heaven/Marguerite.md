> *"Lost love is still love, Eddie. It takes a different form, that's all... Life has to end. Love doesn't."*

## Biography

### Courtship
Eddie met Marguerite when he was 17, at the Stardust Band Shell. They danced to "You Made Me Love You." She had olive skin, dark coffee eyes, and a warmth that melted Eddie's gruff exterior. His brother Joe teased him relentlessly, but Eddie was smitten.

Before Eddie left for the war, Marguerite told him: *"You don't have to ask me to wait."*

### Marriage
They married on Christmas Eve in a modest Chinese restaurant. The accordion player sat on a stool. Chairs had to be carried downstairs after the ceremony. Years later, Marguerite would joke that the only thing missing was bingo cards.

Their love was **grateful, deep, and quiet** — the kind of love Eddie knew was irreplaceable.

### The Accident
Eddie's gambling at the racetrack caused a fight. Marguerite drove to find him. Teenagers dropping a whiskey bottle from an overpass smashed her windshield. She crashed into a divider, suffering severe injuries including a damaged liver and a broken arm. She was bedridden for six months.

The medical costs and delay cost them the **adoption** they had been planning. The child they were expecting went to someone else. An unspoken blame shadowed their marriage for years.

### Reconciliation
Over time, their wound healed. They talked again. They walked the boardwalk. One evening they ate grape popsicles on the beach and realized they were the oldest people there — and happy.

### Illness & Death
At 47, while breading chicken cutlets, Marguerite's hand locked open. A brain tumor was discovered. She endured treatments, radiation, and vomiting. In her final days, she asked to go home. She looked at the distant Ferris wheel from their apartment window and said:

> *"Home."*

She was 47.

## Connection to Eddie

Marguerite is the **fourth person** Eddie meets in heaven. She appears as she looked on their wedding day — young, beautiful, in a lavender dress. Eddie collapses in tears. They walk through endless wedding receptions (her chosen heaven) and talk about everything.

She reveals that she has been waiting for him. She also met five people after her death, and then waited for Eddie.

## The Lesson

> [!note] Lesson 4: Love
> *"Lost love is still love, Eddie. It takes a different form, that's all. You can't see their smile or bring them food or tousle their hair or move them around a dance floor. But when those senses weaken, another heightens. Memory. Memory becomes your partner. You nurture it. You hold it. You dance with it. Life has to end. Love doesn't."*

Eddie spent years after her death feeling that his life was over. Marguerite teaches him that his love for her continued, even in silence. And she felt it — even in heaven.

## The Dance

Marguerite saves one wedding reception for last — a dimly lit room with foldable chairs and an accordion player. Eddie asks her to change her appearance from the young bride to the woman she was at the end. She does. They dance to their song, and Eddie finally says:

> *"I don't want to go on. I want to stay here."*

She vanishes, leaving him with the memory.
