# Tala

> *"Is where you were supposed to be, Eddie Main-ten-ance."*

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Tala |
| **Age at Death** | 5 years old |
| **Nationality** | Filipino |
| **Cause of Death** | Burned alive in a hut during the American escape |
| **Heaven** | A river where children play and wash themselves |

## Biography

Tala was a small Filipino child living in a village that became a war zone. When soldiers approached, her mother told her to hide inside the **nipa** (a hut) and wait. Tala obeyed. But American soldiers — Eddie, Morton, and the others — set the village huts on fire to destroy the enemy camp. Tala was inside one of them.

Eddie thought he saw a shadow moving in the flames. He tried to run inside. The Captain shot him to stop him. Eddie never knew the shadow was real.

For decades, Eddie suffered nightmares — flames, squealing, a shadow crawling. He thought it was guilt, trauma, madness. It was actually **memory**.

## Connection to Eddie

Tala is the **fifth and final person** Eddie meets in heaven. She appears as a small girl on a white boulder by a river, surrounded by thousands of playing children.

At first, Eddie makes her a pipe-cleaner dog, just as he did for children at Ruby Pier. Then she speaks the devastating words:

> *"You burn me. You make me fire."*

Eddie collapses. All his nightmares were true. He killed a child. He howls, confesses, and weeps until drained.

## The Washing

Tala asks Eddie to wash her. She removes her embroidered blouse, revealing horribly burned skin — blackened, charred, blistered. Eddie takes a stone and scrubs her scars. The singed flesh peels away. Healthy skin emerges. Her plum-colored hair grows back. When she opens her eyes, their whites flash like beacons.

> *"I am five,"* she whispers. Then she holds up five fingers and pushes them against his chest: **your five. Your fifth person.**

## The Lesson

> [!note] Lesson 5: Purpose
> Eddie's life was not wasted. His job at Ruby Pier kept thousands of children safe. Every bolt he tightened, every brake he checked, every ride he inspected prevented accidents. Tala tells him:
> *"Children. You keep them safe. You make good for me. Is where you were supposed to be, Eddie Main-ten-ance."*

## The Final Revelation

Eddie asks about the little girl at Ruby Pier — Amy or Annie — the one he died trying to save. Did he save her?

Tala shakes her head. **"No pull."** Then she says:

> **"Push. Push her legs. No pull. You push. Big thing fall. You keep her safe."**

Eddie didn't pull the girl — he **pushed** her out of the way. And the hands he felt? Not the girl's hands. **Tala's hands.** She brought him to heaven. She kept him safe.

## Transformation

The river rises, engulfing Eddie. Tala pulls him through colors — blue, ivory, lemon, black — the emotions of his life. He emerges above an unimaginable scene: Ruby Pier filled with thousands of children, past, present, and future, all there because of the simple, mundane things he did every day. Their voices meld into a single word from God:

> **Home.**

He floats up to the Ferris wheel, where Marguerite waits with open arms.
