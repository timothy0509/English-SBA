# Lesson 5: Purpose

> [!note] The Lesson
> Eddie's life had profound meaning because he kept children safe. His "meaningless" job of maintenance was a sacred responsibility. Every bolt tightened, every brake checked, every ride inspected prevented harm and allowed joy.

## Teacher

**[[Tala]]** — The Filipino child Eddie accidentally killed in the war

## Context

Eddie's final heaven is a river where children play. A small girl named Tala stands on a white boulder. She is the shadow Eddie saw in the burning hut — the child he couldn't save, the source of his decades of nightmares.

She tells him: *"You burn me. You make me fire."*

Eddie collapses in confession and grief. Then she asks him to wash her. As he scrubs away her burn scars with a stone, she teaches him his final lesson.

## The Lesson Explained

### 1. Eddie's Job Mattered
Tala tells him:
> *"Children. You keep them safe. You make good for me. Is where you were supposed to be, Eddie Main-ten-ance."*

Every day at Ruby Pier, Eddie protected thousands of children he never met.

### 2. Making Good
Tala says Eddie's work at the pier "made good" for her death. He could not undo burning her. But he spent 40 years preventing other children from coming to harm. His life balanced his sin.

### 3. The Final Revelation
Eddie asks about the girl at Ruby Pier — did he save her?

Tala reveals: Eddie didn't pull her. He **pushed** her legs. The cart fell. She was safe.

And the hands he felt? **Tala's hands.** She brought him to heaven. She kept him safe.

### 4. The Vision
Eddie sees Ruby Pier filled with thousands of children — past, present, and future — all there because of his simple, faithful work. Their voices become a single word:

> **Home.**

## Real-World Application

- "Small" jobs often have enormous unseen impact.
- Faithfulness in ordinary work is a form of heroism.
- Your purpose may not be glamorous, but it is vital.
- Redemption is possible through service.

## Key Quote

> *"Supposed to be there."* — pointing to Ruby Pier

> *"Not her hands. My hands. I bring you to heaven. Keep you safe."*
