# Theme - Love and Loss

> *"Lost love is still love, Eddie. It takes a different form, that's all... Life has to end. Love doesn't."*

## Definition

Death does not end love. It transforms it into memory, which becomes a living partner. The pain of loss is real, but the love itself continues.

## Evidence from the Text

### Eddie and Marguerite
Eddie never stopped loving Marguerite after her death. He shooed away birthday celebrations because they reminded him of her. He visited her grave last at the cemetery. He thought about taffy. In heaven, she tells him she felt his love the entire time.

### Eddie's Mother
After her husband's death, she continued talking to him, cooking for two, fluffing both pillows. Her love outlived his presence.

### The Captain's Men
They drifted apart after the war, unable to speak of what they'd seen. But the bond remained.

### Eddie's Final Vision
At the end, Eddie sees Ruby Pier filled with thousands of children — all there because of his love, expressed through maintenance and protection.

## Why It Matters

Grief is universal. The book offers comfort:
- Love does not have an expiration date.
- Memory is not a consolation prize — it is love itself, transformed.
- The people we lose are still part of us.
