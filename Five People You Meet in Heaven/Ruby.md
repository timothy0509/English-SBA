# Ruby

> *"Holding anger is a poison. It eats you from inside."*

## Biography

Ruby grew up poor, forced to leave school at 14 to work as a waitress at the Seahorse Grille. She was attractive and turned away many proposals. Then a wealthy, charming businessman named **Emile** walked in. He courted her extravagantly, and they fell in love by the sea.

Emile promised to build her an amusement park that would capture their happiness forever. He made a deal with a railroad company and constructed a magnificent pier, importing carousels from France and Ferris wheels from Germany. He named it **Ruby Pier**, with a grand entrance featuring her painted face beneath a dome.

### The Fire

On Independence Day, roustabouts set off fireworks near the park. The wind carried sparks; everything was lathe and tar. The pier burned. Emile ran into the flames trying to save his creation and was crushed by a collapsing column. He survived but was broken in body and spirit. He sold the charred grounds for far less than their worth. Ruby never wanted to see the pier again.

## Connection to Eddie

Ruby never met Eddie on Earth. Yet she is his **third person** because their lives were deeply intertwined:

- Her husband built the pier where Eddie worked.
- Her husband died trying to save it — the same pier that trapped Eddie.
- Most crucially: **Emile shared a hospital room with Eddie's father.** Ruby witnessed Eddie's father's final nights.

She reveals the truth Eddie never knew:

### The Truth About Eddie's Father

[[Mickey Shea]] had attacked Eddie's mother. Eddie's father chased him to the ocean, intending to kill him — but instead **saved him from drowning**. He lay on the beach for hours in the cold rain, and contracted the pneumonia that killed him. His silence afterward was shame, not cruelty. And in his final night, he woke from a coma, crawled to the hospital window, and called out the names of his family.

## The Lesson

> [!note] Lesson 3: Forgiveness
> *"Holding anger is a poison. It eats you from inside. We think that hating is a weapon that attacks the person who harmed us. But hatred is a curved blade. And the harm we do, we do to ourselves."*

Ruby tells Eddie he must forgive his father — not for his father's sake, but for his own. Eddie's anger trapped him at the pier just as much as his father's death did.

## Transformation

Eddie kneels before the ghost of his father in the diner corner booth. He whispers, "I was angry with you, Dad. I hated you." He pounds the table, slumps to the floor, and finally says the old familiar words:

> *"It's fixed."*

When he looks up, Ruby has become young and beautiful again. She opens the door and lifts into the jade sky, her work done.
