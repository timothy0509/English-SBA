# Dominguez

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Young maintenance worker at Ruby Pier |
| **Personality** | Optimistic, enthusiastic, fond of Eddie |
| **Plans** | Trip to Mexico with his wife Theresa |

## Role in the Story

Dominguez is one of the few living characters who sees Eddie's death and its aftermath. He represents **the next generation** and **Eddie's quiet legacy**.

### Before the Accident
- Wishes Eddie happy birthday.
- Tells Eddie about his upcoming trip to Mexico.
- Eddie gives him forty dollars to buy his wife something nice.
- They share a running joke about fishing through a hole in the boardwalk, though they never catch anything.

### After the Death
Dominguez is devastated. He bursts through the crowd, moaning, "Ahh no, no, Eddie." The next morning, he comes to work early, turns on the sink, then turns it off — the silence without Eddie is unbearable.

### The Funeral
Dominguez is asked to share Eddie's "unique qualities" with the pastor. He says simply:

> *"Eddie really loved his wife."*

### Inheriting the Job
Dominguez agrees to take over Eddie's job. He packs Eddie's few possessions into a trunk at the maintenance shop, alongside Ruby Pier memorabilia. He puts Eddie's things "where it was, as if he were coming back tomorrow."

## Significance

Dominguez shows that Eddie **was noticed and loved**, even if he didn't realize it. Through Dominguez's eyes, we see the hole Eddie leaves behind.
