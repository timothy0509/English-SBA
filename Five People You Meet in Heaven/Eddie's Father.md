# Eddie's Father

> *"That boy raised a hand to me."*

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Eddie's father |
| **Occupation** | Maintenance worker at [[Ruby Pier]] |
| **Personality** | Silent, violent when drunk, neglectful, yet capable of loyalty |
| **Death** | Pneumonia, after saving Mickey Shea from drowning |

## Relationship with Eddie

### Neglect
As an infant, Eddie was rarely held. As a child, his father would take him to the pier, then pawn him off on acrobats or animal trainers while he drank and played cards. Eddie waited hours for attention that rarely came.

### Violence
When drunk, Eddie's father lashed his sons with a belt, screaming that they were wasting his money. Eddie learned to read his father's footsteps in the hallway to predict how bad the beating would be.

### Silent Affection
Occasionally, pride cracked through:
- He nodded when Eddie hit a baseball well.
- He approved when Eddie won fights defending his brother.
- He told Eddie to be his brother's keeper.
- His dirty fingernails, held up with a grin, were his wordless approval.

### The Silence
After Eddie returned from the war, his father demanded he "get a job." When Eddie defended himself — grabbing his father's arm mid-swing — the old man never spoke to him again. Not at Eddie's wedding. Not when Eddie visited his mother. His only explanation: *"That boy raised a hand to me."*

## The Hidden Truth

Through [[Ruby]], Eddie learns what really happened:

[[Mickey Shea]] had attacked Eddie's mother. Eddie's father chased him to the ocean intending to kill him. But he couldn't let an old friend drown. He dragged Mickey from the surf and lay on the beach for hours in the freezing rain. Pneumonia set in. He died from **loyalty**, not drunkenness.

In his final night, he woke from a coma, crawled to the hospital window, and called out for his wife, his sons, and even Mickey.

## Significance

Eddie's father represents:
- **Generational trauma**: The damage fathers pass to sons
- **Unexpressed love**: Affection shown through silence and work, not words
- **The cost of loyalty**: He died saving a man who had betrayed him
- **Forgiveness**: Eddie must forgive him to move on
