# War in the Philippines

## Context

Eddie enlisted in the U.S. Army during World War II and fought in the Philippines. The war fundamentally changed him — physically, emotionally, and spiritually. It is the defining trauma of his life.

## Key Events

### Enlistment
Eddie shaved, combed back his hair, and enlisted early one rainy morning. His mother didn't want him to go. His father asked only, "When?" Eddie practiced shooting at the Ruby Pier arcade, firing metal slugs at pictures of jungle animals.

[[Mickey Shea]] gave him harsh advice: *"Fire and you fire and you don't think about who you're shootin' or killin' or why... It's the thinking that gets you killed."*

### Captivity
Eddie, the Captain, Smitty, Morton, and Rabozzo were captured and held in a bamboo barracks on stilts above muddy ground. They were forced to mine coal for the enemy war effort.

**Conditions:**
- Rice balls filled with salt
- Brownish broth with grass and dead hornets
- One cup of water every few hours
- Sleeping on burlap sacks stuffed with straw
- Guards called "Crazy One, Crazy Two, Crazy Three, Crazy Four"

### Rabozzo's Murder
Rabozzo, a redheaded kid from Portland, developed a severe skin rash and diarrhea. When he collapsed in the mine, Crazy Two mocked him, then shot him in the head. After that, Eddie stopped praying and focused only on escape.

### The Escape
Eddie distracted the guards by juggling rocks. When three guards gathered to watch, the Americans attacked, killed all four captors, and fled.

### The Burning Village
The men set the enemy huts on fire. Eddie thought he saw a child-sized figure inside a burning barn. He tried to run in. Morton couldn't stop him. The Captain shot him in the leg.

The truth — revealed decades later by [[Tala]] — was that there **was** a child inside: Tala, hiding where her mother told her to wait.

### The Captain's Death
As the transport fled, the Captain jumped out to clear a gate. He stepped on a land mine and was blown apart.

### Eddie's Injury
The bullet shattered Eddie's knee, cutting nerves and tendons. Two surgeries failed to fix it. Doctors said he'd limp for life, and it would worsen with age. They were right.

## Significance

The war is the wound Eddie never heals from:
- It killed his dream of being an engineer.
- It gave him the limp that trapped him at Ruby Pier.
- It left him with nightmares, darkness, and the inability to feel joy.
- It made him feel he "lost himself" and came home a different man.
- It hid his greatest sin (killing Tala) until heaven revealed it.
