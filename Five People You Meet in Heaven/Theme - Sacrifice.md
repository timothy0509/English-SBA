# Theme - Sacrifice

> *"Sometimes when you sacrifice something precious, you're not really losing it. You're just passing it on to someone else."*

## Definition

Sacrifice is not a tragedy to be regretted, but a natural and noble part of life. Every person gives up something for another — time, health, dreams, even life itself. The value of sacrifice is not in what is lost, but in what is gained by others.

## Evidence from the Text

### The Captain's Sacrifice
He gave his life clearing a path for his men. He also gave Eddie's leg to save Eddie's life. He never regretted it because it kept his promise: "No one gets left behind."

### Rabozzo's Sacrifice
He died in the coal mine, but his death inspired his younger brother to become "a good soldier and a great man."

### Eddie's Father's Sacrifice
He saved Mickey Shea from drowning, knowing Mickey had attacked his wife. The act cost him his health and eventually his life.

### Eddie's Sacrifice
He gave up his dream of engineering, his mobility, his chance at fatherhood, and ultimately his life — all to protect others.

## Why It Matters

The book reframes sacrifice from "loss" to "gift." It suggests:
- Parents sacrifice for children.
- Soldiers sacrifice for countries.
- Ordinary people sacrifice their ambitions for family.
- These are not failures — they are the fabric of a meaningful life.
