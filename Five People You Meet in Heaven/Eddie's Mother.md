# Eddie's Mother

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Eddie's mother |
| **Personality** | Warm, plump, tender, devoted to her family |
| **Key Trait** | The primary source of affection in Eddie's childhood |

## Role in the Story

Eddie's mother is the counterbalance to his father. Where his father was cold and violent, she was soft and loving:
- She baked vanilla cakes with blue candles for birthdays.
- She danced with Eddie in the living room.
- She walked him along the pier and told him God was proud of him.
- She defended Eddie when his father was cruel.

After her husband's death, she was devastated. She spoke to him as if he were still there — yelling at him to turn down the radio, cooking for two, fluffing both pillows. When Eddie gently told her, "Dad's gone," she asked, "Gone where?"

Her need for care was one reason Eddie took the maintenance job at Ruby Pier and never left.

## Key Moments

- **Birthday Dances**: She danced with Eddie to swing music on the radio, twirling him despite being a foot shorter.
- **Hospital Vigil**: She slept at her husband's bedside every night, moaning, "I should have done something."
- **Aftermath**: She retreated into a daze of grief, unable to accept his death.

## Significance

She represents **unconditional maternal love** — the warmth that kept Eddie from being completely broken by his father. But her dependence after her husband's death also trapped Eddie at the pier.
