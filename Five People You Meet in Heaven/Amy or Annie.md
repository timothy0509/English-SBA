# Amy or Annie

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Unknown — Eddie shouts either "Amy!" or "Annie!" as he dies |
| **Role** | The little girl at Ruby Pier whom Eddie pushes to safety |
| **Significance** | Her survival proves Eddie's death was meaningful |

## The Accident

On Eddie's 83rd birthday, a cart on Freddy's Free Fall drops after its cable snaps. Eddie sees a small girl standing in its path. He lunges, grabs her legs, and pushes her out of the way. The cart strikes him instead.

In his final moment, Eddie feels two small hands in his. He assumes they are hers. In heaven, [[Tala]] reveals: **"Not her hands. My hands."**

## Aftermath

The novel's epilogue confirms the girl survives. She will grow up, and one day she too will die and meet five people in heaven. The last image is Eddie waiting in line for her, holding his pipe-cleaner animals, finally at peace.

## Significance

Amy/Annie represents the **unknown lives we save** without ever knowing their names. Eddie died believing his life was wasted, but his final act was the culmination of 40 years of protecting children at Ruby Pier.
