## Biography

Emile was a wealthy businessman who fell in love with Ruby, a waitress at the Seahorse Grille. He courted her extravagantly and promised to build her an amusement park that would capture their happiness forever.

He partnered with a railroad company seeking weekend passengers and constructed a magnificent pier on the ocean. He imported carousels from France and Ferris wheels from Germany. The entrance was modeled after a historic French temple, with fluted columns and a coved dome. He painted Ruby's face on the entrance, beneath a sign reading: **"Ruby Pier: ADMISSION—ONE DIME."**

## The Fire

On Independence Day, fireworks sparks ignited the tar-and-lathe construction. Emile ran into the flames to save what he had built and was crushed by a collapsing column. He survived but was broken in body and spirit. He sold the damaged grounds for far less than their worth and never recovered.

## Connection to Eddie

Emile never met Eddie. But without Emile, there would be no Ruby Pier. Without Ruby Pier, Eddie would not have met Marguerite, would not have worked there for decades, and would not have died saving a child. Emile is part of the invisible chain of connection that defines the novel.
