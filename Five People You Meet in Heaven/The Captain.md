# The Captain

> *"I took your leg to save your life."*

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Never stated |
| **Role** | Eddie's commanding officer in the U.S. Army |
| **Background** | Three generations of military; disciplined upbringing |
| **Famous Promise** | "No one gets left behind" |
| **Cause of Death** | Stepped on a land mine while clearing a path for his men |
| **Heaven** | The Philippine hills — but transformed into lush, pre-war paradise |

## Biography

The Captain came from a long military line. He learned to fire a pistol at age six. His father bounced quarters on his bedsheets. Before the war, he only took orders; suddenly, he was giving them to frightened young men who believed he could keep them alive.

He wasn't perfect — he had a short temper, yellowed teeth from tobacco, and yelled inches from soldiers' faces. But he kept his promise: **no one gets left behind.**

## The War & Captivity

The Captain led Eddie, Smitty, Morton, and Rabozzo in the Philippines. They were captured and held in a bamboo POW camp for months, forced to mine coal. Conditions were brutal: rice balls with salt, brown broth with grass, dead hornets in the soup.

When Rabozzo was murdered by a guard, the Captain and Eddie planned escape. Eddie used his juggling skills to distract the guards, and the Americans killed their captors and fled.

## The Shooting

During the escape, Eddie became obsessed with a shadow he saw inside a burning hut — convinced a child was trapped inside. He tried to run into the flames. Morton couldn't stop him. The Captain couldn't wrestle him down. With moments to spare before the area was bombed, the Captain made a split-second decision:

> He shot Eddie in the leg.

Eddie fell, the others dragged him out, and they escaped. The Captain's intention: a leg wound would heal. Burning alive would not.

Eddie never knew the truth. He spent decades believing his injury was a mistake, a random tragedy, or his own fault.

## The Land Mine

After loading wounded Eddie into a transport, the Captain ran ahead to clear a gate. He stepped on a land mine and was blown to pieces. Eddie never knew how he died.

## Connection to Eddie

The Captain is the **second person** Eddie meets in heaven. Eddie is furious when he learns the Captain shot him. They fight — Eddie pounds the Captain's face, but the Captain does not bleed or resist. He lets Eddie have his rage, then explains:

> [!note] Lesson 2: Sacrifice
> *"Sacrifice is a part of life. It's supposed to be. It's not something to regret. It's something to aspire to... Sometimes when you sacrifice something precious, you're not really losing it. You're just passing it on to someone else."*

## The Lesson in Depth

- The Captain sacrificed his own life clearing the path for his men.
- He sacrificed Eddie's leg to save Eddie's life.
- Rabozzo sacrificed his life for his country, inspiring his younger brother.
- Sacrifice creates a chain: one person loses so another can gain.
- Eddie's anger came from focusing on what he lost, not what was given to him.

## Transformation

The Captain reveals that the barren battlefield is not what he sees. With a wave of his hand, the rubble melts, trees grow, the sky turns sapphire, and the island becomes pristine beauty — the world before war. He no longer smokes. "Why would I smoke in heaven?"

He vanishes after Eddie finally offers his hand in forgiveness.
