# Ruby Pier

> *"The end of the line. That's where I work."* — Eddie

## Description

Ruby Pier is an amusement park on a great gray ocean. It is Eddie's workplace, his prison, his legacy, and — in a sense — his home.

### Golden Age
In the 1920s–1930s, Ruby Pier was magnificent:
- Elephants, fireworks, marathon dance contests
- The Parisian Carousel (imported from France)
- A grand entrance based on a historic French temple, with fluted columns and a coved dome
- The original Ferris wheel (from Germany)
- Wooden ticket booths, canvas tents, sideshows
- Chowder for 10 cents

It was built by [[Ruby]]'s husband Emile as a gift to her, in partnership with a railroad company seeking weekend riders.

### Decline
Over the decades, the pier faded:
- Old rides torn down (Stardust Band Shell, Zipper, Tunnel of Love)
- People stopped coming to ocean piers; they went to theme parks with $75 tickets and furry mascots
- The park became a shadow of its former self, kept alive by workers like Eddie

### Modern Era (at Eddie's death)
- Freddy's Free Fall (later renamed Daredevil Drop)
- Ghoster Coaster
- Squiggly Wiggly Worm Adventure
- Pipeline Plunge
- Bumper cars, taffy stands, arcade

## Ruby Pier as a Character

The pier is more than a setting — it mirrors Eddie's life:

| Pier | Eddie |
|------|-------|
| Once magnificent | Once young and full of promise |
| Gradually decayed | Grew old, bitter, and arthritic |
| Still operational despite wear | Still working despite feeling worthless |
| Hidden dangers beneath the surface | Hidden pain beneath his gruff exterior |
| Kept safe by Eddie's vigilance | Kept meaningful by unnoticed acts |

## Key Events at Ruby Pier

- **Eddie's birth** (1920s, nearby hospital)
- **Eddie's childhood birthdays** (5th, 7th, 8th, 17th, 33rd, 37th, 38th, 39th)
- **Eddie meets Marguerite** at the Stardust Band Shell
- **Eddie returns from the war** and takes the maintenance job
- **Eddie's father's collapse** on the boardwalk
- **Eddie's death** at Freddy's Free Fall

## After Eddie's Death

The park reopened three days later. Freddy's Free Fall was renamed Daredevil Drop. Eddie's apartment was rented to someone new who put leaded glass in the kitchen window, obscuring the carousel view. But the children kept coming — because Eddie had kept them safe.
