# Joe

## Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Role** | Eddie's older brother |
| **Personality** | Talkative, competitive, outwardly successful, less tough than Eddie |
| **Career** | Salesman for a hardware company (later moved to Florida) |
| **Fate** | Died of a heart attack at age unknown, a month after buying a condominium |

## Relationship with Eddie

Joe and Eddie had a classic brotherly rivalry:
- Joe teased Eddie mercilessly about Marguerite.
- Joe wore Joe's hand-me-downs and got the new things.
- Joe was the swimmer; Eddie was the grease-stained worker.

Their father judged them differently: Joe "ain't tough enough for anything but water." Eddie was the strong one, the protector.

As adults, Joe became a salesman with a salary three times Eddie's. He showed off battery-operated drills and talked about his grandson. Eddie felt the gap but never expressed resentment openly.

## Significance

Joe represents **the road not taken** — the more conventionally successful life Eddie might have had. But Joe's death (before Eddie's) also reminds us that worldly success does not guarantee happiness or longevity.

## Key Moments

- Teasing Eddie about Marguerite at Eddie's 17th birthday, leading to a fistfight.
- Showing off his new drill and salary at Eddie's 38th birthday.
- Calling Eddie from Florida on his birthdays in old age.
