# Theme - Purpose and Meaning

> *"Children. You keep them safe. You make good for me. Is where you were supposed to be, Eddie Main-ten-ance."*

## Definition

Purpose is not found in fame, wealth, or grand achievements. It is found in the cumulative effect of small, faithful actions. Eddie's "meaningless" life of checking bolts and tightening brakes saved thousands of children.

## Evidence from the Text

### Eddie's Job
Every day, Eddie walked the park, checked for broken boards, loose bolts, worn-out steel. He listened to the machinery and could "hear trouble." To him, it was dull. To the children who rode safely, it was everything.

### The Final Vision
In heaven, Eddie sees Ruby Pier filled with thousands of children — past, present, and future — all there because of his unnoticed work.

### The Contrast with Joe
Joe had a better salary, a condominium, a grandson. But Eddie's life had deeper meaning because it protected others.

### Tala's Forgiveness
Tala tells Eddie that his work at Ruby Pier "made good" for her death. His life balanced his sin. His purpose was redemption.

## Why It Matters

Modern culture often measures worth by status and wealth. The book argues:
- A "small" life can have enormous impact.
- Faithfulness in ordinary work is heroic.
- You may never know how many lives you touch.
- Your purpose is not what you achieve, but who you protect.
