# 06 - Study Guide

Use this guide to test your understanding of the novel. Answers are hidden in foldable callouts — try to answer before revealing.

## Character Questions

> [!question] Who are the five people Eddie meets in heaven, and what is each person's connection to his life?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > 1. **The Blue Man** — Joseph Corvelzchik; Eddie caused the car accident that killed him as a child.
> > 2. **The Captain** — Eddie's commanding officer who shot him in the leg to save him from a fire.
> > 3. **Ruby** — Namesake of Ruby Pier; shared a hospital room with Eddie's father and witnessed his final nights.
> > 4. **Marguerite** — Eddie's beloved wife.
> > 5. **Tala** — The Filipino child Eddie accidentally burned alive in the war.

> [!question] Why did Eddie's father stop speaking to him?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > After Eddie returned from the war, his drunken father demanded he get a job and tried to punch him. Eddie grabbed his father's arm — the first time he ever defended himself. His father never spoke to him again, telling anyone who asked: "That boy raised a hand to me."

> [!question] What is the truth about Eddie's father's death that Ruby reveals?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Eddie's father chased Mickey Shea to the ocean after Mickey attacked Eddie's mother. Instead of killing Mickey, he saved him from drowning and lay on the beach for hours in the rain, contracting the pneumonia that killed him.

## Plot Questions

> [!question] How does Eddie die?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > On his 83rd birthday, a cart on Freddy's Free Fall drops because of a frayed cable (caused by Nicky's lost car key). Eddie pushes a little girl out of the way and is killed by the impact.

> [!question] What caused the accident at Freddy's Free Fall?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Months earlier, a teenager named Nicky lost his car key at the park. It fell through an opening and wedged in a pulley, causing the cable to fray over time until it snapped.

> [!question] Did Eddie save the little girl at the pier?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Yes, but not the way he thought. He didn't pull her — he pushed her legs out of the way. And the hands he felt were not hers; they were Tala's hands, bringing him to heaven.

## Lesson Questions

> [!question] What is the first lesson, and who teaches it?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > **Connection**, taught by the Blue Man. All lives are interconnected; there are no random acts. Fairness does not govern life and death.

> [!question] What is the second lesson, and why is the Captain the one who teaches it?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > **Sacrifice**, taught by the Captain. He sacrificed Eddie's leg to save his life, then sacrificed his own life clearing a path for his men. Sacrifice is part of life and should be aspired to, not regretted.

> [!question] What is the third lesson, and how does Ruby's personal history relate to it?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > **Forgiveness**, taught by Ruby. Her husband built Ruby Pier, which trapped Eddie in a maintenance job. She witnessed Eddie's father's suffering and revealed the truth, enabling Eddie to forgive him. Anger is a poison that harms the holder.

> [!question] What is the fourth lesson, and what setting does Marguerite choose for her heaven?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > **Love**, taught by Marguerite. She chooses a world of endless wedding receptions because marriage represents hope and possibility. She teaches Eddie that lost love is still love, transformed into memory.

> [!question] What is the fifth lesson, and how does Tala transform Eddie's understanding of his life?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > **Purpose**, taught by Tala. Eddie believed his life was wasted. Tala reveals that his maintenance job kept thousands of children safe. His life "made good" for her death.

## Theme Questions

> [!question] How does the novel challenge traditional ideas of heaven?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Heaven is not a paradise of clouds and rivers. It is a place of understanding, where five people explain your life to you. Each person's heaven is different, based on their chosen memories.

> [!question] What does Ruby Pier symbolize?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Ruby Pier mirrors Eddie's life: once magnificent, gradually decayed, but still operational. It represents the idea that ordinary places and jobs have hidden meaning and importance.

> [!question] Why does the novel use birthday flashbacks?
> > [!faq]- Answer
> > Birthdays mark the passage of time and highlight the contrast between Eddie's hopes and his reality. They also show how the same day can hold joy and tragedy simultaneously.

## Discussion Questions

- Do you agree that "fairness does not govern life and death"?
- Is sacrifice always noble, or can it be destructive?
- Can you forgive someone who never apologized?
- Does memory truly keep love alive?
- What "small" jobs in society have the biggest hidden impact?
