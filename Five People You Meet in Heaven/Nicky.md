## The Accident Chain

Months before Eddie's death, Nicky visited Ruby Pier with friends. Uncomfortable carrying a key chain, he removed his single car key and put it in his jacket pocket, then tied the jacket around his waist.

After riding the fastest rides, he discovered the key was gone. It had fallen through an opening at a precise moment, wedging itself in a pulley inside Freddy's Free Fall. For months, the locked pulley scraped the cable, gradually severing the steel wires.

On Eddie's 83rd birthday, the cable snapped. The cart dropped. Eddie died.

## Aftermath

Nicky made a new key when he got home. He sold his car four months later. He returned often to Ruby Pier, where he bragged to friends that his great-grandmother was the woman for whom it was named — [[Ruby]].

## Significance

Nicky embodies the novel's central theme of **invisible connection**. He never knew Eddie. Eddie never knew him. Yet Nicky's careless moment set in motion the events that killed Eddie — and, in doing so, gave Eddie the chance to save a little girl and discover the meaning of his life.

> *"No story sits by itself. Sometimes stories meet at corners and sometimes they cover one another completely, like stones beneath a river."*
