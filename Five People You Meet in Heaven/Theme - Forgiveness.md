# Theme - Forgiveness

> *"Holding anger is a poison. It eats you from inside... hatred is a curved blade. And the harm we do, we do to ourselves."*

## Definition

Forgiveness is not about excusing wrongdoing — it is about releasing the poison of anger that harms the victim more than the perpetrator. Understanding the full context of someone's actions makes forgiveness possible.

## Evidence from the Text

### Eddie and His Father
Eddie hated his father for neglect, violence, and silence. In heaven, Ruby reveals that his father died saving Mickey Shea — an act of loyalty, not drunkenness. His father's silence was shame, not cruelty. Eddie finally kneels and whispers, "It's fixed."

### Mickey Shea
He never forgave himself for attacking Eddie's mother and causing his friend's death. He drank himself to the grave.

### The Captain
He asks Eddie to forgive him for shooting him in the leg. Eddie does, and the Captain is freed to move on.

### Eddie and Himself
Eddie's deepest anger may be at himself — for his leg, for his "wasted" life, for Tala. Forgiving his father is a step toward forgiving himself.

## Why It Matters

Anger is a prison. The book argues:
- You cannot move forward while holding grudges.
- Understanding someone's full story creates compassion.
- Forgiveness heals the forgiver more than the forgiven.
